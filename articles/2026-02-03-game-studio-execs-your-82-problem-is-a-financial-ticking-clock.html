The Hidden Reality

Stop the Damage / Secure the Win

  • Model new financial scenarios that include potential union-negotiated wages, benefits, and work rules.
  • Review all contractor and outsourcing agreements for stability if internal labor conditions change.
  • Prioritize transparency on project roadmaps and crunch policies to proactively address top grievances.
  • Consult with legal counsel specializing in labor law to understand your rights and obligations.

The High Cost of Doing Nothing

You will be blindsided. The first studio to unionize will set the market rate for talent, forcing you into reactive, costly catch-up. You'll face project delays from potential labor actions, skyrocketing unforeseen personnel costs that gut your profit margins, and a permanent loss of trust that drives your best developers to unionized competitors, crippling your ability to execute.
